# Corporate Gifting Strategy - Chocolat on James

## Executive Summary

Corporate gifting represents a high-value, underserved opportunity for Chocolat on James. This strategy outlines the path to building a corporate gifting program that generates $50-100K in Year 1 and $130K+ by Year 2, with excellent profit margins and predictable revenue.

**Current State**: No corporate program, ad-hoc corporate sales only  
**Year 1 Target**: $50,000-75,000 corporate revenue (7-11% of total)  
**Year 2 Target**: $130,000 (14% of total)  
**Key Advantage**: Local Hamilton presence with same-day delivery

---

## Strategic Rationale

### Why Corporate Gifting

**1. High-Value Transactions**
- Average order: $300-2,000 (vs. $50 retail)
- Fewer transactions for same revenue
- More efficient use of time

**2. Predictable Revenue**
- Annual repeat clients (70-80% retention)
- Seasonal concentration (plan ahead)
- Less price-sensitive than retail
- Budget allocations made in advance

**3. B2B Relationships**
- Long-term client relationships
- Referrals common in business networks
- Multiple decision-makers become advocates
- Corporate clients also become retail customers

**4. Local Advantage**
- Hamilton businesses prefer supporting local
- Personal service vs. national providers (Purdy's, online)
- Same-day delivery capability
- Face-to-face relationship building
- Custom solutions easier

**5. Revenue Smoothing**
- Corporate orders in slower months
- Extends beyond December peak
- Quarterly corporate events
- Employee recognition programs

## Product & Pricing Strategy

- **Product**: Small gift box (6-8 pieces)
- **Use Case**: Thank you gifts, small appreciations
- **Target**: Realtors, small businesses, individual orders
- **Margin**: 70%

**Tier 2: Standard - $50-100**
- **Product**: Medium gift box (12-16 pieces) or custom selection
- **Use Case**: Client gifts, employee appreciation
- **Target**: Most corporate clients
- **Margin**: 72%
- **Most Popular Tier**

**Tier 3: Premium - $100-200**
- **Product**: Large gift box (24+ pieces) or luxury selection
- **Use Case**: Top clients, executive gifts, special occasions
- **Target**: Professional services, high-value relationships
- **Margin**: 73%

**Tier 4: Custom/Bulk - $200+**
- **Product**: Fully customized, branded, bulk orders
- **Use Case**: Major clients, large employee groups, events

- **Margin**: 65-68% (volume discount applied)

## Sales & Marketing Approach

### Corporate Sales Process

**Stage 1: Prospecting & Outreach**

**Identify Target Companies**:
- Research Hamilton businesses
- LinkedIn Company Search
- Hamilton Chamber of Commerce member list
- Business directories
- Networking event attendees

**Build Target List**: 100-150 qualified prospects

**Outreach Methods**:

1. **LinkedIn Connection + Message**
   - Personal connection request
   - Follow-up with corporate program info
   - Share case studies/examples
   - Offer consultation

2. **Direct Email Outreach**
   - Professional email template
   - Subject: "Support Local: Corporate Gifting from Chocolat on James"
   - Brief introduction
   - Value proposition
   - Call-to-action (schedule call, request catalog)

3. **Phone Calls**
   - Follow-up to email
   - Warm introduction
   - Understand gifting needs
   - Schedule meeting or send samples

4. **In-Person Networking**
   - Chamber of Commerce events
   - Business networking groups
   - Industry association meetings
   - Hamilton business mixers

5. **Referrals**
   - Ask current corporate clients for introductions
   - Offer referral incentive (10% credit)
   - Leverage personal network

**Stage 2: Discovery & Consultation**

**Initial Meeting** (15-30 minutes):
- Understand their gifting needs
- Current gifting approach and budget
- Decision-making process
- Timeline (quarterly, annual, ad-hoc)
- Customization requirements
- Quantity and frequency

**Propose Solutions**:
- Recommend product tiers
- Provide pricing options
- Explain customization possibilities
- Share delivery logistics
- Offer samples (for qualified prospects)

**Stage 3: Sampling**

**Sample Strategy**:
- Send complimentary sample box to decision-maker
- Include corporate brochure and pricing
- Follow-up call 3-5 days after delivery
- "Did you enjoy the chocolates? Let's discuss your needs."

**Cost**: $15-25 per sample  
**Conversion Rate**: 30-50% of sampled prospects  
**ROI**: High (one client = 10-40 samples worth)

**Stage 4: Proposal & Close**

**Create Custom Proposal**:
- Specific product recommendations
- Pricing with volume discount
- Delivery schedule
- Customization details
- Payment terms
- Timeline

**Address Objections**:
- "We already use Purdy's" → "Support local, better service, same-day delivery"
- "Too expensive" → "Volume discounts, quality justifies price, local impact"
- "Need to think about it" → "Let's start with a trial order for one occasion"

**Close Techniques**:
- Trial offer: "Let's do your next quarterly thank-yous and see how it goes"
- Seasonal urgency: "Book your holiday gifts now for best selection and priority delivery"
- Consultation: "I'll create a custom proposal for you - no obligation"

**Stage 5: Fulfillment & Service**

**Onboarding**:
- Confirm order details in writing
- Delivery logistics coordination
- Recipient list management
- Invoicing arrangement (Net 30 for qualified clients)

**Execution**:
- Quality control on corporate orders (Dan's personal review)
- Coordinate delivery (batch or individual)
- Tracking and confirmation
- Problem resolution (immediate attention)

**Follow-Up**:
- Thank you note
- Request feedback
- Request testimonial/referral
- Offer repeat order discount

**Stage 6: Retention & Growth**

**Annual Planning**:
- Schedule annual planning meeting (Q3)
- Review past year's orders
- Forecast upcoming year needs
- Lock in holiday orders early
- Discuss budget for next year

**Account Management**:
- Quarterly check-ins
- Remind of key gifting occasions
- Offer seasonal specials
- Easy reordering process
- VIP treatment

**Upsell Opportunities**:
- Additional occasions (quarterly → monthly)
- Larger orders (10 → 25 recipients)
- Premium tiers (standard → premium boxes)
- Additional services (custom branding)

## Operational Considerations

### Corporate Order Management

**Order Intake**:
- Email orders: corporate@chocolatonjames.com
- Phone orders: Dedicated line or extension
- Online form: Quote request form
- In-person: Consultation meetings

**Order Processing**:
- Confirmation email within 4 hours
- Invoice generation (Net 30 for approved clients, otherwise prepayment)
- Production planning (coordinate with retail demands)
- Quality control (Dan reviews all corporate orders personally)
- Delivery coordination

**Delivery Logistics**:
- **In-Hamilton**: Dan or staff delivers personally (relationship building)
- **Bulk deliveries**: All boxes to one location (office reception)
- **Individual recipients**: Coordinate addresses, deliver individually or ship
- **Tracking**: Update client on delivery status
- **Confirmation**: Photo proof of delivery (for large orders)

### Payment Terms

**New Clients**:
- Prepayment required (credit card, e-transfer, cheque)
- Net 15 after first successful order

**Established Clients** (after 2+ successful orders):
- Net 30 payment terms
- Invoice on delivery
- Monthly statements
- Auto-pay option

**Large Orders** ($2,000+):
- 50% deposit to confirm order
- Balance due on delivery or Net 30

### Staffing for Corporate

**Year 1** (under $75K corporate revenue):
- Dan manages personally
- Admin support (2-5 hours/week) for order processing
- Production team makes products (no additional staff needed initially)

**Year 2** ($130K corporate revenue):
- Consider part-time corporate sales coordinator (10-20 hours/week)
- Handles inquiries, order processing, delivery coordination
- Dan focuses on relationship management and closing
