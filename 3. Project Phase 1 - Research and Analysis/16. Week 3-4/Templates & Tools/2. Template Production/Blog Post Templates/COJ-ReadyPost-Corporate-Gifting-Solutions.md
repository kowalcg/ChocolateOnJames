## What Makes a Corporate Gift Stand Out

Three things separate a gift that gets a polite thank-you from one that genuinely impresses:

**1. Authenticity**
Mass-produced chocolate is forgettable. Artisanal chocolate—made with real ingredients, with a story behind it—is different. When your gift comes with context (where the cacao came from, the craft behind it), the recipient understands the thought that went in. That matters.

**2. Personalization**
A generic box of assorted chocolates is nice. A curated selection based on what you know about the recipient—their taste, their preferences—is better. That's the difference between a gift and *the* gift. We work with corporate clients to build custom boxes: dark chocolate for the purist, fruity notes for someone adventurous, a mix for teams where tastes vary.

**3. Presentation**
Packaging matters not because it's showy, but because it signals quality. When a gift arrives in a beautifully designed box with care instructions or tasting notes, the recipient knows they've received something considered. It doesn't have to be elaborate—it just has to show intentionality.

## Corporate Gifting Scenarios

**Client Appreciation**
Send a thank-you gift after closing a deal or renewing a partnership. A curated box of 8–12 pieces ($35–$60) works perfectly. Include a personalized card with your company name. The client remembers the moment they unwrapped it—and by extension, they remember you.

**Employee Recognition**
Recognize milestone moments—promotions, anniversaries, work anniversaries—with a gift that feels personal without being awkward. A Chocolat on James box says "we see your contribution" without overstepping. Budget: $20–$40 per person.

**Holiday Corporate Gifts**
November and December are prime time. Corporate clients love building a program: small gifts for all clients/employees around the holidays. We've helped companies set up tiered gifting—everyone gets something, top clients get premium selections. It's efficient, it's thoughtful, and it sets the tone for the year ahead.

**Event Hospitality**
Hosting a conference, trade show, or networking event? Chocolate favours create positive associations. Guests feel cared for, and they remember your brand. Custom-wrapped pieces with your logo can be wrapped at scale without losing the "handcrafted" feel.

**Team Building and Incentives**
Looking to celebrate a team win or incentivize performance? A communal tasting experience—where teams gather to taste and discuss different chocolates—builds connection and breaks up routine. It's memorable in a way that a standard lunch isn't.
