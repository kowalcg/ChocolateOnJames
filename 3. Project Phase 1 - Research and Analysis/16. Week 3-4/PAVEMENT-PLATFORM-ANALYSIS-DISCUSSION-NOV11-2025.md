## Critical Issues Identified

### 1. CATALOG LIMITATIONS

**What Pavement Said:**
- "Our system isn't built for LARGE SKU libraries (200+ items) as most quick service spots work best with smaller skus"
- Setup includes "Max 50 Items" initially

**Critical Questions:**
- After they set up 50 products, can Dale add more himself?
- Or does every product addition count against the 50 annual updates?
- What's the actual SKU threshold where performance degrades? (50? 75? 100?)

**Why It Matters:**
- Chocolate shop needs: truffles (30+ flavors), bars, seasonal items, gift boxes, corporate options
- Easily 75-100+ SKUs for proper growth
- Seasonal rotation essential (Valentine's, Easter, Christmas)
- Can't wait 2 weeks every time you need to add a product

### 2. CONTENT/PAGE LIMITATIONS

**What Pavement Said:**
- "Initial Website Design & Setup (Max 5 Pages)"
- Updates require 2-week lead time

**Critical Questions:**
- After initial 5 pages, can Dale add more pages himself?
- Or does every new page (blog, landing page, gift guide) count against 50 annual updates?
- Can he publish blog content for SEO?

**Why It Matters:**
- Need 15-20+ pages for proper e-commerce (policies, FAQs, about, gift guides, seasonal pages)
- SEO/organic growth depends on adding educational content regularly
- Content marketing is essential for growth strategy
- Can't wait 2 weeks to publish a blog post or seasonal landing page

### 3. LAYOUT/DESIGN LOCKED

**What Pavement Said:**
- "The layout isn't customizable by design, but the style & content is fully customizable (ie you can change the paint color and hanging art, but you can't change the frame)"

**Reality:**
- What launches November 17 is the template structure you keep
- Cannot evolve site design as business grows
- Limited ability to optimize for conversions
- Stuck with their template forever

### 4. UPDATE BOTTLENECK

**The Constraint:**
- 50 updates per year = ~4 per month
- 2-week lead time for each update
- Only 5 hours of one-on-one support annually (~25 min/month)

**Critical Questions:**
- What counts as an "update"?
- Can Dale change prices, mark sold-outs, update descriptions himself?
- Or does everything go through their queue?

**Why It Matters:**
- Growing businesses need agility
- Can't wait 2 weeks to respond to market demand
- Seasonal businesses need rapid product changes
- 50 updates/year is severely limiting if it includes routine changes

### 5. EMAIL MARKETING SEVERELY LIMITED

**What Pavement Said:**
- "Customer Email Updates & Marketing (1 email per action per order)"

**What This Likely Means:**
- Transactional emails only (order confirmation, shipping notification)
- NO marketing campaigns
- NO newsletters
- NO promotional emails
- NO automated flows (welcome series, abandoned cart, win-back)

**Critical Questions:**
- Can Dale send actual marketing campaigns and newsletters?
- Can he build and segment an email list?
- Can he connect external email platform (Klaviyo, Mailchimp)?
- Can he export customer email addresses?

**Why It Matters:**
- Email marketing typically delivers 15-20x ROI (highest performing channel)
- My Phase 1 strategy projected:
  - 500+ email subscribers by Month 4
  - Automated welcome series (3 emails)
  - Abandoned cart recovery (3 emails)
  - Regular promotional campaigns (2-4 per month)
  - Seasonal campaigns
- Without email marketing, growth projections are not achievable

### 6. "TASTY LOCAL" MARKETING IS MINIMAL

**What I Discovered:**
- Tasty Local is a brand new startup (launched May 2025 - only 6 months old)
- Hamilton-only food directory/ordering platform
- Created by same founder as Pavement (Peter Lazar)
- Unknown subscriber count (estimated 1,000-2,000 people max)

**What "5 Mentions Per Year" Really Means:**
- 5 features in their newsletter/social annually
- Going to a very small, local audience
- Could be one-line mentions in roundup emails
- This is NOT marketing - it's minimal promotional support
- Don't count on this for growth

### 7. CORPORATE B2B FEATURES COST EXTRA

**What Pavement Said:**
- "We can build out an entire corporate login with account options and new menus if necessary"
- "Around $500-$1k depending on complexity"

**Critical Questions:**
- Is this one-time or ongoing costs?
- What exactly is included for that price?
- Can platform handle multiple corporate accounts with different pricing?
- What's the scalability for B2B growth?

**Why It Matters:**
- Corporate gifting projected at $50-75K Year 1 (critical revenue stream)
- Need tiered pricing, bulk ordering, custom invoicing, net-30 terms
- If every B2B feature costs extra, total platform costs could be much higher than $79/month

### 8. ANALYTICS & TRACKING UNCLEAR

**What Pavement Said:**
- "We offer endpoints for custom integrations to hundreds of POS / Accounting / CRM integrations via automation software"
- "Backups are taken daily, Data Exports are available as CSV/JSON from our dashboard"

**Critical Questions:**
- Can Dale install Microsoft Clarity, Google Tag Manager, Google Analytics 4 directly?
- Or is he limited to whatever basic analytics Pavement provides?
- Can he add custom tracking pixels and conversion tracking?

**Why It Matters:**
- Need proper analytics to optimize conversion rates
- Understanding customer behavior is essential for growth
- Data-driven decisions require comprehensive tracking
- Can't improve what you can't measure

## Consultant Notes

### My Assessment

**Pavement Strengths:**
- Fast launch (November 17)
- Simple pricing ($79/month base)
- Food-business focused
- Local Hamilton connection (Tasty Local)
- They handle technical setup

**Pavement Weaknesses:**
- Service model = less control
- Update bottleneck (50/year, 2-week wait)
- Catalog limitations (not built for 100+ SKUs)
- Email marketing appears minimal
- Layout locked forever
- Support minimal (5 hours/year)
- Tasty Local marketing is negligible

**Comparison to Original Plan:**
- Phase 1 plan was built around Shopify
- Shopify = full control, unlimited scaling, robust ecosystem
- Pavement = managed service with significant constraints
- May need to significantly adjust expectations

### Recommended Approach

**If constraints are severe:**
1. Accept platform limitations
2. Plan conservatively within those constraints
3. Build "owned assets" separately (email list, content library)
4. Use Pavement for basic e-commerce
5. Supplement with external tools where possible
6. Plan for potential platform migration in 12-18 months

**If constraints are manageable:**
1. Understand the workarounds
2. Adjust workflows to work within their system
3. Focus on what you CAN control
4. Execute growth strategy with modified tactics
5. Monitor if platform supports growth trajectory

### Key Principle

**Don't blame the platform - work with what you have.**

But also: **Know what you have so you can plan realistically.**

The goal isn't to prove Pavement is bad - it's to understand capabilities so we deliver achievable results for Dale.
