### Email 4: January 14 (Tuesday)
**Chocolate Tasting 101 - How to Taste Like a Pro**

**Subject Line:** Chocolate Tasting 101 - Taste Like a Pro

**Preview Text:** Discover the 5 steps to truly experiencing chocolate

---

Hi [FIRST NAME],

Did you know most people eat chocolate too fast to actually taste it?

Today, we're sharing the secrets professional chocolate tasters use - and you can do this at home with any of our bars.

**The 5-Step Tasting Process:**

**1. Look**
Notice the color and shine. Quality chocolate has a glossy surface and even color.

**2. Listen**
Break the bar. You should hear a clean "snap." This means it was tempered correctly.

**3. Smell**
Hold it near your nose. What do you notice? Fruity? Earthy? Nutty? This is the "bouquet."

**4. Taste**
Let it melt on your tongue. Don't chew yet. Notice how the flavors develop and change.

**5. Feel**
Pay attention to the texture. Is it smooth? Creamy? Does it coat your mouth?

[IMAGE: Tasting setup with chocolate pieces]

**Try It Yourself**
Our Tasting Flight includes 4 different chocolates with tasting notes. Perfect for practicing at home.

Quick question: Why chocolate for Valentine's Day?

The answer goes back further than you'd expect.

**A Brief History:**

**The Aztecs (1400s)**
They called chocolate "the food of the gods" and believed it was an aphrodisiac. Montezuma reportedly drank 50 cups a day.

**European Royalty (1600s)**
When chocolate arrived in Europe, it was expensive and exotic - perfect for romantic gifts between nobility.

**Cadbury (1861)**
Richard Cadbury created the first heart-shaped chocolate box. He was a marketing genius.

**Today**
Over $2 billion in chocolate is sold for Valentine's Day in North America alone.

---

Spring cleaning time!

**Chocolate Storage Spring Cleaning:**

**Check Your Stash:**
Look for:
- White spots (sugar bloom) - still safe, just cosmetic
- Gray streaks (fat bloom) - still safe, texture may be off
- Off smells - this is a bad sign
- Hard/crumbly texture - past its prime

**Proper Storage Reminder:**

**DO:**
- Store at 65-68°F
- Keep in airtight container
- Away from strong smells
- Dark place preferred

**DON'T:**
- Refrigerate (unless necessary)
- Store near spices or coffee
- Leave in direct sunlight
- Keep near heat sources
